How Taxes Work in Magento 2 Store and What Tax Configurations are Available: Taxes have always been a complex issue, and businesses seek to automate their calculations to make it easier for managers and improve the shopping experience for customers. Adobe’s platform-based online solution offers rule-based tax calculation automation through the Magento system. It applies charging rules based on customer information, item classes, customer shipping and billing addresses, and store location.

In Magento 2, a manager can set up multiple item classes to separate taxes, based on the item information, customer information, charge zones, and rates. The store’s admin interface provides the following tax sections for easy configuration and management.

Tax Rules: 

The tax rules comprise a combination of client and item classes and respective expense rates. Each client is assigned to a tax class, and each item is allocated to a tax class. The system analyzes each client’s order and calculates the appropriate tax based on the client and item classes, as well as the area determined by the client’s shipping, billing, or delivery address.

Tax Classes: 

Tax classes can be assigned to clients, items, and shipping services. The system examines each customer’s order and calculates the tax based on the customer’s class, the ordered items, and the customer’s district, which is determined by their shipping, billing, or delivery address. New tax classes can be created when introducing tax rules.

Fixed Product Tax: 

Some local districts have a mandatory fee that should be added to specific items. Based on the store’s tax calculations, a manager can set up this charge as a fixed value (FPT). For example, in some countries, FPT may be used to set up a WEEE tax, also known as an environmental tax or eco-tax. This type of fee is collected on certain electronics to offset the cost of recycling old electronic devices and is a predefined amount rather than a percentage of the item cost.

The Price Display Setting:

determines whether the displayed price for an item and its shipping costs include the tax amount. It’s possible to show two versions of the price, one with and one without tax.

Tax Zones and Rates:

set the tax rate for each geographic region where taxation is collected. The manager can indicate the rate for each region and assign an identifier, allowing different rates for the same region if necessary, such as regions that exempt certain items like food or medication.

Value Added Tax (VAT):

is a tax charged on goods and services in some countries, with rates that vary based on the stage of production or distribution. In some cases, multiple VAT rates may be used for tax calculation purposes.

The Tax Quick Reference

provides options for determining how taxes are calculated and presented to the customer. The Hidden Tax conditions determine the amount of VAT that a discount has, and is non-zero when catalog prices include tax, the VAT rate is not zero, and a discount is present.

International Tax Guidelines 

apply to the income earned from overseas operations and sales, with tax treaties between countries determining which country collects the tax revenue, and anti-avoidance rules limiting opportunities to minimize the global tax burden.

Vertex Cloud:

is a cloud-based solution that automates sales tax compliance and generates a signature-ready PDF for monthly returns. A Vertex account is required, with configuration options available for tax settings, address cleansing for the store, country overrides, and performance-enhancing options.
