How to add Google Analytics code to Magento 2?

Published on: February 01, 2023

The success of an e-commerce store is dependent on various factors, with traffic being a major one. It’s crucial for store owners to track and monitor their store traffic, and Google Analytics provides a free solution to do so. With this service, store owners can monitor traffic and conversion rates, and evaluate user engagement on every page to improve Magento SEO. If you’re wondering how to add Google Analytics code to Magento 2, how to add Google Analytics code in Magento 2, or even how to set up Google Analytics 4 Magento 2, you’re in the right place. This article will guide you through the process of adding Google Analytics code to your Magento 2 store.

Boost Your E-Commerce Insights: How to Add Google Analytics Code to Magento 2 with This Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Create a Google Analytics Account

  • Log in to your Gmail account and search for Google Analytics.
  • Click on Analytics from the top corner of the page.
  • Click on Sign Up.
  • Enter the details of your store (Account Name, Website Name, and Website URL) and click on Get Tracking ID.
  • Read the Google Analytics Terms of Service Agreement and click on I Accept.

Note – If you’re currently using Universal Analytics to track your Magento 2 store traffic, it’s important to note that Google has recently declared the end of Universal Analytics. As of July 1, 2023, it will no longer process new data. This means that your time to migrate to Google Analytics 4 is limited, making it your only option. To learn how to add Google Analytics code to Magento 2, check out our guide above. And for more information on this transition, you can visit this site.

Step 2: Set up a Property in Google Analytics

  • Create a property in Google Analytics for each website you want to collect data for.
  • After creating a property, your Google Analytics ID will be generated for future setup.
  • Follow instructions from Google.

You will be redirected to the Tracking ID page. Copy the Tracking ID for use in the configuration step.

Step 3: Add Google Analytics Code to Magento 2

  • Log in to the Admin panel of your store and go to Stores â†’ Configuration.
  • Scroll down the configuration page and click on Google API under the SALES tab.
  • In the Google Analytics section, select Yes from the Enable drop-down menu, enter the Tracking ID from Step 1 in the Account Number field, and select Yes from the Enable Content Experiments drop-down menu.
  • Click on Save Config.

Step 4: Enable Ecommerce Tracking for Google Analytics

  • In the Admin area of Google Analytics, click on Ecommerce Settings.
  • Enable E-commerce and Enhanced E-commerce Reporting.
  • Click on Submit.

And you’re done! Check your store traffic activity in Google Analytics by visiting your store and then your Google Analytics account.
