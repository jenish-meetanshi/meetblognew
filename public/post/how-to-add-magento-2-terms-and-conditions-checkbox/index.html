How To Add Magento 2 Terms And Conditions Checkbox

Published on: June 15, 2023


Magento 2 is an advanced e-commerce platform that offers businesses a flexible and expandable solution for managing their online stores. When it comes to running an online store, one crucial aspect to consider is establishing clear terms and conditions that customers must agree to before completing a purchase. These terms and conditions serve to safeguard both your business and your customers, minimizing the potential for legal conflicts down the line.

By default, the checkout process in Magento 2 does not include a checkbox for accepting the terms and conditions. However, incorporating this feature is a straightforward procedure that can be accomplished in just a few steps. In this blog post, we will guide you through the process of seamlessly learning How to add Magento 2 Terms and Conditions Checkbox.

Steps to Add Magento 2 Terms and Conditions Checkbox

Step 1: Create a New Checkbox Attribute

The first step in adding a Magento 2 terms and conditions checkbox to your Magento 2 store is to create a new checkbox attribute. This attribute will be used to store the customer’s agreement to the terms and conditions. To create a new attribute, follow these steps:

1. Log in to your Magento 2 admin panel.

2. Navigate to Stores > Attributes > Customer.

3. Click the Add New Attribute button.

4. Fill in the required fields, such as Attribute Code and Default Value.

5. Under the Catalog Input Type for Store Owner dropdown, select Yes/No.

6. Click the Save Attribute button to create the new attribute.



Step 2: Add the Checkbox to the Checkout Page

The next step is to add the checkbox to the checkout page. To do this, follow these steps:

1. Navigate to Stores > Configuration > Sales > Checkout.

2. Under the Checkout Options section, find the Enable Terms and Conditions checkbox and set it to Yes.

3. Under the Terms and Conditions section, enter the text of your terms and conditions in the respective fields.

4. Under the Checkbox Text field, enter the text you want to display next to the checkbox.

5. Under the Checkbox Checked by Default dropdown, select No.

6. Under the Checkbox Custom Attribute field, enter the attribute code you created in Step 1.

7. Click the Save Config button to save your changes.



Step 3: Test the Checkbox

The final step is to test the checkbox to ensure that it is working correctly. To test the checkbox, follow these steps:

1. Go to your store’s checkout page.

2. Add a product to your cart and proceed to the checkout page.

3. Check the box next to the terms and conditions checkbox.

4. Click the Place Order button to complete your purchase.

5. If the purchase is successful, then the checkbox is working correctly, and customers must agree to the terms and conditions before making a purchase.



Additional Tips

Here are some additional tips to consider when adding a terms and conditions checkbox to your Magento 2 store:

1. Keep your terms and conditions clear and concise. Customers are more likely to read and agree to terms and conditions that are easy to understand.

2. Make sure your terms and conditions comply with all relevant laws and regulations.

3. Consider adding a link to your terms and conditions on your store’s homepage or footer.

4. Update your terms and conditions as needed. If your business changes, your terms and conditions may need to be updated as well.

Conclusion

Incorporating a terms and conditions checkbox into your Magento 2 store is vital for safeguarding your business and customers. By adhering to the guidelines provided in this blog post, you can promptly implement this functionality into your store’s checkout process. This addition allows customers to effortlessly accept your terms and conditions prior to making a purchase, significantly reducing the likelihood of future legal disputes. It is crucial to maintain clear and concise terms and conditions, ensuring compliance with pertinent laws and regulations, and regularly updating them to guarantee accuracy.
