How to Configure Braintree Payment Method in Magento 2

Published on: July 05, 2023

If you are looking to enable Braintree Payment Method in your Magento 2 store, it is important to first check that your business type is supported and you are located in one of the eight countries where the platform is available. Once confirmed, follow the steps on how to configure Braintree Payment Method in Magento 2 by enabling the Magento Secure Payment Bridge and properly setting it up. To help you get started, we’ve provided a guide on How to Configure Braintree Payment Method in Magento 2. With this guide, you’ll be able to easily accept payments from multiple checkout options offered by Braintree such as credit/debit cards, PayPal, Apple Pay, Android Pay, Venmo, Bitcoin, and more.

How to Configure Braintree Payment Method in Magento 2: A Step-by-Step Guide

Step 1: Initiate Braintree Payment Setup

  • Go to Stores > Settings > Configuration from the Admin sidebar.
  • Under the panel on the left, select Sales > Payment Methods.
  • Expand the Braintree section and click the Configure button to initiate the setup.

Step 2: Configure Basic Braintree Settings

1. Go to the Basic Braintree Settings section and either log in or sign up for a new Braintree account.

2. Enter a title, or leave the default “Credit Card (Braintree)”.

3. Set the environment to “Sandbox” for testing or “Production” for live transactions.

4. Choose “Authorize Only” or “Authorize and Capture” in the Payment Action field.

5. Enter your Braintree credentials (Merchant Account ID, Merchant ID, Public Key, Private Key).

6. Enable the Braintree payment method by setting “Enable this Solution” to “Yes”.

7. Enable PayPal as a payment option by setting “Enable PayPal through Braintree” to “Yes”.

Step 3: Configure Advanced Braintree Settings

1. Set Debug to “Yes” to save interaction logs between your store and Braintree.

2. Choose “Invoice” or “Shipment” in the Capture Action field.

3. Set the New Order Status to “Processing” or “Suspected Fraud”.

4. Enable vault storage by setting “Use Vault” to “Yes”.

5. Set “Allow Duplicate Cards” as desired.

6. Require CVV verification by setting “CVV Verification” to “Yes”.

7. Choose accepted credit card types in the “Credit Card Types” list.

8. Enable credit card auto-detection on the storefront by setting “Enable Credit Card auto-detection on Storefront” to “Yes”.

9. Apply for Braintree fraud protection by setting “Advanced Fraud Protection” to “Yes”.

10. Improve performance with caching by setting “Use Cache” to “Yes”.

11. Determine the position of Braintree in the checkout payment methods list by entering a number in the “Sort Order” field (starting from 0).

Step 4: Configure Country-Specific Settings

  • Under the Payment from Applicable Countries field, select “All Allowed Countries” to allow Braintree Payment Method for customers from all countries specified in your store configuration or “Specific Countries” to limit this payment method to certain countries selected from the Payment from Specific Countries list.
  •  For the Country Specific Credit Card Types section:
  • Click the Add button.
  • Choose a country and select the Allowed Credit Card Type from the list.
  • Repeat to specify the credit cards accepted for each country.

Step 5: Configure PayPal through Braintree Settings

1. Title: Enter a custom title or keep the default, “PayPal (Braintree),” to display Braintree’s PayPal payment option on the checkout page.

2. Sort Order: Enter a number to set the position of Braintree’s PayPal payment in the checkout payment method list (start from 0).

3. Override Merchant Name: Enter a custom name to display a different merchant name than the specified in-store configuration.

4. Payment Action: Choose “Authorize” to hold funds for capture by the merchant or “Authorize and Capture” to authorize and immediately withdraw from the customer’s account.

5. New Order Status: Set to “Processing” or “Suspected Fraud.”

6. Payment from Applicable Countries: Choose “All Allowed Countries” to accept Braintree’s PayPal for customers from all countries specified in-store configuration or “Specific Countries” to approve for selected countries.

7. Require Customer’s Billing Address: Set to “Yes” to require a billing address.

8. Display on Shopping Cart: Set to “Yes” to display the PayPal button in the mini cart and shopping cart page.

9. Allow to Edit Shipping Address: Set to “Yes” to enable customers to edit shipping addresses during PayPal checkout.

10. Debug: Set to “Yes” to save a log of interactions between your store and PayPal through Braintree.”

Step 6: Configure 3D Verification Options

Set 3d Secure Verification to “Yes” to implement an additional verification step for customers who use credit cards enrolled in programs such as Verified by VISA.

When finished, click the Save Config button in the upper-right corner.
