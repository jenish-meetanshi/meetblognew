Streamlining Magento 2 with CDN: A Comprehensive Walkthrough

What Is a CDN And Why E-commerce Sites Need One?

How to speed up Magento 2 is a crucial aspect to consider for online success, as customers expect quick website loading and a seamless experience. The speed of a Magento 2 store is largely determined by the hosting provider and the location of its data center. For example, hosting with a provider like GoDaddy or SiteGround in a North American data center will result in faster load times for users in the same region. However, those visiting the site from further away, such as in Asia, may experience slower performance.

One effective way to speed up Magento 2 is by utilizing a Content Delivery Network (CDN). CDNs improve the delivery of content and data, enhancing the overall user experience for Magento 2 e-commerce stores, eliminating slow load times, and ensuring seamless performance for all visitors, regardless of their location.

How CDN works?

Setting up a Content Delivery Network (CDN) is an essential step for enhancing the performance of a Magento 2 store. A CDN operates by utilizing a network of servers placed strategically across the globe, which hold a cached version of the website’s content. This network of servers collaborates to transfer important assets such as product images, CSS and JavaScript files, videos, and more, ensuring faster load times and seamless operations.

By hosting the cached content on a CDN, data is transmitted at lightning-fast speeds, reducing page load times considerably. But the benefits of CDNs extend far beyond quick page loading times. With the right configuration, a CDN can also attract traffic from multiple platforms like YouTube and Facebook, and protect against spam and malicious attacks.

In conclusion, CDNs play an instrumental role in enhancing the customer experience for online businesses and merchants. Knowing how to set up a Content Delivery Network (CDN) in a Magento 2 store is crucial for maximizing the potential of your online business and keeping your customers happy.

Steps to Configure a Content Delivery Network in Magento 2

Here are the steps you need to follow to configure a Content Delivery Network (CDN) in your Magento 2 store:

  1. Go to the Admin panel and click on “Stores”. Then, select “Configuration” in the Settings section.
  2. In the panel on the left, select “Web” under “General”.
  3. Open the “Base URLs” section and follow these instructions:
  • In the “Base URL for Static View Files” field, enter the URL of the location on the CDN where static view files are stored.
  • In the “Base URL for User Media Files” field, enter the URL of the JavaScript files on the CDN.

Once you have completed the above steps, click on “Save Config”. Note that all of the fields mentioned above can be left blank, or they can start with the placeholder “{{unsecure_base_url}}”.
