What is CAPTCHA?

Are you looking for information on How to Configure Magento 2 CAPTCHA on Your Store? CAPTCHA is an effective tool used to enhance website security by verifying the user’s humanity rather than being a robot. Its functioning involves displaying a distorted image containing a random combination of numbers and letters. Users are then required to enter the characters correctly into a text box. This test, commonly known as the “Completely Automated Public Turing test to tell Computers and Humans Apart,” is extensively utilized by numerous websites, including Magento. Configure Magento CAPTCHA for Admin Panel

Improving the security of your Magento 2 admin panel is made easier with the addition of CAPTCHA for Magento admin login and reset password pages. Magento offers the ability to configure this feature directly from the admin panel, without the need for any integrations.

Here’s how to set up Magento 2 admin panel CAPTCHA:

1.Navigate to Stores > Configuration > Advanced > Admin.

2. Enable CAPTCHA in Admin, choosing the relevant website if you have a multi-website setup.

3. Select the desired font for CAPTCHA in the admin. (You can also upload your own font by defining it in the config.xml file located in the CAPTCHA module at app/code/Magento/Captcha/etc within your Commerce installation.)

4. Choose the forms where CAPTCHA should be enabled.

5. Set the display mode for Magento 2 admin CAPTCHA:

Always: CAPTCHA will be required for every login.

After a number of attempts to log in: CAPTCHA will only appear after a specified number of unsuccessful login attempts.

6. Define the number of unsuccessful login attempts that will trigger the appearance of the Magento CAPTCHA.

7. Define the expiration time for the CAPTCHA in minutes.

8. Set the number of symbols in the CAPTCHA, with a range of 4 to 8 symbols being possible.

9. Choose the symbols to be used in the CAPTCHA, including upper and lower case letters and numbers, which will be randomly generated.

10. Enable the case sensitivity option to require proper casing of the symbols entered by the admin user.

After successfully configuring CAPTCHA, don’t forget to save your settings. It’s important to thoroughly test the CAPTCHA functionality on both the login and password reset pages to ensure it meets your requirements. In case the CAPTCHA image appears unclear to the admin, they can easily reload it to obtain a clearer version. Remember, testing and verifying the setup will help ensure optimal performance and security.

Configure Magento CAPTCHA for Storefront

To set up CAPTCHA in Magento 2, configuring it for the storefront follows a similar process to that of the admin panel. However, there are a few distinctions. Specifically, for the storefront, you have the flexibility to enable CAPTCHA on various forms, including applying a coupon code, checking out/placing an order, creating a user, logging in, resetting passwords, contacting the website, changing passwords, and sharing a wishlist form. The most commonly utilized forms for CAPTCHA implementation contact us, change password, login, and customer creation.

Once you have made the necessary CAPTCHA display mode, symbol, and font configurations, it is important to save your changes and proceed with testing the CAPTCHA functionality on the storefront. Whenever CAPTCHA is enabled, whether it’s on the contact us page, login page, or customer registration page, your customers will be required to successfully complete the CAPTCHA challenge before they can continue browsing your website.
