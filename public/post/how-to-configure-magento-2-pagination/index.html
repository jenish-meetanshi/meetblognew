How to Configure Magento 2 Pagination?

Published on: February 20, 2023

 If you’re looking to improve your online store’s navigation and performance, one of the key features you’ll want to configure is Magento 2’s pagination controls. In this article, we’ll show you how to configure Magento 2 pagination to create an engaging and effective online store.

To get started with how to configure Magento 2 pagination settings, log in to your admin panel and navigate to Stores > Configuration. In the Configuration menu, click on Catalog and then select the Catalog option. Under the “Storefront” section, you’ll see a “Pagination” option. Here, you can configure the number of products displayed per page and the maximum number of products allowed on a single page. You can also choose the number of pages displayed in the pagination controls.

Once you’ve learned how to configure Magento 2 pagination settings , you can further customize the look and feel of your pagination controls to match your store’s branding. Simply go to the Design section under Stores > Configuration and select your theme. From there, navigate to the “Pagination” section and configure the color and style of your controls to make them more user-friendly and visually appealing.

Using Magento 2’s page navigation controls is crucial for simplifying the browsing experience for your customers, especially if you offer a large number of products. By placing these controls at both the top and bottom of your product listing page, your customers can easily access the different pages of products. This improves the user experience and makes it easier for customers to find the products they’re looking for.

How to Configure Magento 2 Pagination for Improved Navigation and Performance.

There are 4 types of Magento pagination controls. You can refer to the table below for more details. 

Magento offers 4 types of pagination controls to enhance the browsing experience for your customers. The following table provides a closer look at each control:

View As: Allows customers to view products in either a list or grid format.

Sort By: Enables customers to change the sorting order of products, which you can configure in the Storefront Properties section when setting up product attributes.

Show Per Page: Specifies the number of products displayed on each page.

Pagination Links: Facilitates navigation between different pages of products.

How to Configure Magento 2 Pagination?

Step 1: Log in to the Magento 2 Admin Panel

The first step in configuring Magento 2 pagination is to log in to the Magento 2 Admin Panel. You will need to enter your admin username and password to access the panel.

Step 2: Go to Stores > Configuration > Catalog > Catalog

Once you have logged in, navigate to the Catalog configuration section. This can be found by going to Stores > Configuration > Catalog > Catalog.

Step 3: Set the Number of Products per Page

Under the Catalog configuration section, scroll down to the Products per Page on the Grid Allowed Values section. Here, you can enter the number of products you want to display per page. You can enter multiple values separated by a comma, for example, 10, 20, 30. This will give your customers the option to choose the number of products displayed per page.

Step 4: Set the Default Sort Order

Next, scroll down to the Default Product Listing Sort By section. Here, you can select the default sort order for your product listings. You can choose from options such as Name, Price, or Position. This will ensure that your products are sorted in a way that makes sense for your online store.

Step 5: Display the Total Number of Products in a Category

In the Display Product Count section, you can choose whether or not to display the total number of products in the category. This can be useful information for your customers, and it can help them understand how many products are available in each category.

Step 6: Save Your Changes

Finally, click the Save Config button at the top right of the page to save your changes. Your Magento 2 pagination settings will now be updated, and your customers will be able to navigate your product lists more easily.
