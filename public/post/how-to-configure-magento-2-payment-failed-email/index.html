How to Configure Magento 2 Payment Failed Email?

Published on: March 17, 2023

In this article, we will provide a detailed guide on how to configure Magento 2 payment failed email and explain why it’s crucial for the success of your online store.

How to Configure Magento 2 Payment Failed Email: A Comprehensive Guide

Customize Failed Payment Email Template

Before setting up the payment failed to email in Magento 2, it’s necessary to make sure that your SMTP Email is properly set up for the task. Here’s how to set up SMTP Email for Magento 2:

1. Enable the SMTP extension.

2. Go to Marketing > Email Templates and create a new template to customize the payout failed email with your brand’s touch. You can personalize the template by adjusting the variables and adding your own design elements to the letter.

With a properly set up SMTP Email for Magento 2, you can ensure that payout failed notifications are delivered efficiently and effectively to your customers.

Configure Magento 2 Payment Failed Email

1.Navigate to Stores > Configuration > Sales > Checkout > Payment Failed Emails.

2. Specify the Payment Failed Email Sender who will appear as the sender of the notification. You can choose from various options available in the dropdown menu, such as General Contact or Sales Representative.

3. Determine the Payment Failed Email Receiver who will receive the notification about the failed payment. This can be the store owner, customer service representative, or any other relevant person.

Note: To set up emails for senders and receivers, make sure to configure Magento store email addresses.

4. Choose the Payment Failed Template that will be used for the notification. You can select from the available options or create a custom template.

5. Set the email address or addresses to Send the Payment Failed Email Copy To. If there are multiple recipients, separate them with a comma.

6. Determine the Payment Failed Copy Method for the email copy. You can choose from the options of None, Separate Email, or Add to Parent.
