How to Configure Newsletters in Magento 2?

Published on: May 08, 2023

Discover how to use Magento’s newsletter feature to keep your customers informed about special deals, sales, and updates in your store with this informative article titled How to Configure Newsletters in Magento 2. Email marketing can be a valuable tool for enhancing sales and building relationships with customers, and Magento simplifies newsletter management directly from the admin interface.

How to Configure Newsletters in Magento 2: A Comprehensive Guide

Step 1: to manage newsletters on your Magento store is to configure newsletter subscription options. Follow these steps:

  •  Go to Stores > Configuration > Customer > Newsletter.
  •  Enable newsletters on your store in General Options.
  •  Set “Allows Guest Subscription” to Yes, if you want unregistered users to subscribe.
  •  Choose whether the newsletter subscription needs to be confirmed by the user.
  •  Choose the confirmation email sender and template, success email sender and template, and unsubscription email sender and template.
  •  Ensure compliance with GDPR and CCPA regulations.

Once these options are set, you can proceed to the next step of adding a new newsletter template.

Step 2: After configuring newsletter subscription options is to create a newsletter template:

  •  Go to Admin Panel > Marketing > Communications > Newsletter Templates and click the “Add New Template” button.
  •  Enter a name and subject for the template.
  •  Set the sender’s name and email address.
  •  Enter the template content using the WYSIWYG editor or HTML.
  •  Design the newsletter with template styles and save the template.

Step 3: After saving the newsletter template, follow these steps to send the newsletter:

  •  Go to the Newsletter management page.
  •  Choose “Queue Newsletter” from the Action dropdown.
  •  Edit the newsletter queue by setting the Queue Date Start and saving the newsletter.
  •  Conveniently access all newsletter information on one page and make changes if needed.

Step 4: In addition to configuring, creating, and sending newsletter templates, Magento allows you to manage newsletter subscribers in two ways:

  • Go to Customers > All Customers, select the customers you want to subscribe or unsubscribe from your newsletter and choose the corresponding option from the Actions dropdown. All selected customers will be automatically subscribed or unsubscribed.
  • Go to Customers > All Customers, select the customer you want to subscribe to, go to the Newsletter section on their page, check the “Subscribed to Newsletter” option at the top of the Newsletter Information section, and save the customer.

When it comes to setting up newsletters in Magento 2, it’s important to remember that subscribing to customers without their consent is not recommended. Instead, encourage customers to sign up on their own, as it can help build a stronger relationship with them. If you subscribe to them without their knowledge, they may be confused as to why they received the newsletter and may end up unsubscribing, which could increase your bounce rates.

By following How to set up Newsletters in Magento 2 and allowing customers to opt-in on their own, you’ll be able to establish a better rapport with your audience. Ultimately, this will lead to higher engagement rates and a more successful email marketing campaign. So, take the time to set up your newsletters properly and watch your business grow.
