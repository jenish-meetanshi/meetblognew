How to Configure Search by SKU in Magento 2: A Step-by-Step Guide

What is an SKU?

SKU, which is short for Stock Keeping Unit, is a distinctive product code comprising 8-12 characters that vendors employ to identify, search for, and track inventory movement.

To maximize the benefits of SKUs, it is crucial to ensure their uniqueness and ease of comprehension. When generating your SKUs, consider the following recommendations:

  1. Ensure that your SKUs are distinct and easily distinguishable from those used by other vendors.
  2. Organize the words in your SKU based on the significance of product variations, such as season, product name, product type, size, and color.
  3. Avoid incorporating letters that resemble spaces, accents, or symbols, as they can lead to confusion.

For instance, let’s suppose you are selling coffee mugs with multiple product variations. To create an SKU for a specific coffee mug, you could utilize the following set of variations: Summer 2019, Zara Tee, Small, and Red. In this case, an appropriate SKU could be S19T-Z-RS.

Why is Search by SKU important?

To optimize the efficiency of your online store, especially when managing a large-scale product inventory, the implementation of SKUs is highly beneficial. By incorporating SKUs, you can effectively handle inventory management, gain valuable product insights, and facilitate quick and convenient product discovery and purchase for your customers. SKUs enable effortless item retrieval, seamless navigation through your product catalog, and prompt identification of out-of-stock items with precision and swiftness. Consequently, integrating SKUs into your store configuration can significantly enhance performance, elevate the customer experience, and ultimately maximize your revenue potential. If you are looking for guidance on How to configure Magento Search by Sku, please find below the steps to follow.

How to configure Search by SKU in Magento 2

  • Log in to your Magento 2 Admin Panel.
  • Go to Stores > Configuration.
  • On the left-hand menu, click on Catalog > Catalog.
  • Expand the Catalog Search section.
  • Set the Search Type to “Like” to enable the SKU search feature and order the results by SKU.
  • Set the Minimum Query Length to the desired number of characters. This specifies the minimum length of the search query that will trigger the SKU search feature.
  • Click on Save Config to save the changes.

After configuring these settings, customers can search for products in your online store by entering the SKU number in the search bar. The SKU search results will be displayed alongside the regular search results and ordered by SKU.
