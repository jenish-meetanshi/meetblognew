How to Enable and Disable Reorders in Magento 2: Improving Customer Experience and Boosting Repeat Business

Retaining customers is easier and more beneficial for marketers compared to acquiring new ones. Repeat customers drive sales and increase website traffic. To keep them coming back, you must offer the best customer experience.

One way to do this is by using Magento 2’s reorder feature, which enables customers to quickly repeat their previous orders with just one click. This eliminates the need for them to search through the entire catalog for previously purchased items, as Magento remembers their previous choices for color, size, and quantity.

Additionally, administrators can also reorder on behalf of customers from the order or customer view pages in the admin panel.

The Magento 2 reorder feature is a crucial aspect of order processing that enhances the customer experience and incentivizes repeat business.

It’s worth mentioning that the reorder option is automatically enabled in Magento 2.

How to Enable and Disable Reorders in Magento 2: A Simple Guide

To Enable or Disable Reorders in Magento 2

1.Navigate to Stores > Configuration > Sales > Sales > Reorders.

2. Enable or disable the Allow Reorders options. 

It is as simple as that. 

When Magento reorders feature is enabled customers can reorder from their account page or order view page.

Once they press the corresponding button, they are redirected to the shopping cart with the ordered items.

With the reorder feature enabled, customers can reorder from their account page or order view page by clicking the reorder button. This will redirect them to the shopping cart with their previously ordered items.

Note: If there were already items in the shopping cart before the reorder button was pressed, they will remain in the cart after the reorder process.

Magento 2 Reorder Isn’t Working

The Reorder button may not appear in a customer account or on the order view page even if the Allow Reorder option is enabled. This doesn’t indicate a malfunction, but rather, certain requirements have not been fulfilled for reordering to function.

The following scenarios may prevent the Magento reorder option from working:

  • The Allow Reorder option is turned off (a common, but fundamental reason).
  • The order status is On Hold or Payment Review.
  • Products from the previous order are unavailable or have been deactivated.
