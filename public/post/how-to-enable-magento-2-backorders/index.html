How to Enable Magento 2 Backorders

Published on: May 17, 2023

If you’re wondering how to enable Magento 2 backorders, you’ve come to the right place. Backorders in Magento allow customers to place orders for products that are currently out of stock, which is a useful feature to prevent customers from abandoning their shopping carts and going to other stores. This feature is particularly beneficial when inventory hasn’t been restocked but you want to maintain customer engagement.

Note – To optimize your stock management and avoid unexpected out-of-stock situations, it is recommended to enable low-stock notifications. This will help you stay informed about your inventory levels and take necessary actions to prevent any potential stock issues.

With Magento 2, you can set up backorders in two different ways – either globally or for a specific product. This article will guide you through the process of implementing both methods and provide an understanding of what backorders are.

Enabling Magento 2 Backorders: A Comprehensive Guide

What are Backorders?

When customers find an item currently unavailable, they have the option to allow backorders for the desired product. By doing so, they can secure the product and have it delivered as soon as it becomes available again, without experiencing delays in restocking.

Allow backorders in Magento can prove to be a valuable tool for store owners, providing insights into the popularity and high demand for specific products. However, it is crucial to emphasize the responsibility of promptly fulfilling these orders once the products are restocked.

What is the Difference Between Backorders and Pre-orders?

There are notable distinctions between backorders and pre-orders, despite their initial similarities. Backorders come into play when an item is temporarily out of stock, allowing customers to secure their purchase for future delivery once the item is restocked. In contrast, pre-orders are reserved for products that have not yet been released, enabling customers to reserve a purchase in advance for when the product becomes available in the store’s offerings.

To summarize, backorders apply to currently unavailable store items, while pre-orders are applicable to forthcoming products that are yet to be launched.

Set Up Magento Backorders Globally 

1.Navigate to Stores > Settings > Configuration > Catalog > Inventory > Product Stock Options.

2. Scroll down to the Backorders field and select one of the available options:

  •  No Backorders: Backorders are disabled.
  •  Allow Qty Below 0: Backorders are allowed when the product quantity falls to 0.
  •  Allow Qty Below 0 and Notify Customer: Backorders are allowed when the product quantity becomes 0, and your customers will be notified about the possibility of placing orders.
  •  Enter a value in the Out-of-Stock Threshold field to specify the number of backorders that can be placed.

Don’t forget to click the Save Config button to save your changes. That’s all it takes to set up backorders globally.

Note – if you input a positive value in the Out-of-Stock Threshold field, backorders will not be allowed when the item runs out of stock. A value of 0 allows an unlimited number of backorders, while a negative value means that backorders will be limited to that number.

Conclusion

Enabling backorders is an excellent way to keep customers engaged even when a particular product is out of stock. With Magento 2, you can set up backorders either globally or for specific products. By configuring backorders globally, you can apply the same rules to all products in your Catalog. If you’re wondering how to enable Magento 2 backorders, you can follow these simple steps.

First, navigate to the Product Stock Options section in the Catalog of your Magento admin panel. Then, select the desired options for backorders, making sure to set an appropriate Out-of-Stock Threshold value. Once you’ve made your selections, remember to save your changes. By completing these steps and Magento 2 enable backorders, you can effectively optimize your stock management and keep your customers satisfied.
