How to fix Unparsable Structured Data Issue in Magento 2 ?

Published on: October 09, 2024

Structured data is crucial for enhancing the visibility of your Magento 2 store in search engine results.

It allows search engines to better understand the content of your pages, which can lead to rich snippetsâ€”enhanced listings that display additional information about your products.

However, issues can arise, particularly with ‘unparsable structured data’, which can hinder your SEO efforts. This blog will guide you through understanding this issue and how to fix it effectively.

What is Unparsable Structured Data?

Unparsable structured data refers to markup that search engines cannot interpret correctly. This can occur due to various reasons, including:

  • Incorrect syntax: Errors in the markup language (like JSON-LD or Microdata).
  • Missing required fields: Essential properties like offers, review, or aggregateRating are absent.
  • Conflicting data: Different types of structured data conflict with each other on the same page.

When structured data is unparsable, it may lead to warnings or errors in Google’s Search Console, impacting your site’s SEO performance.

Importance of Structured Data in Magento 2

Implementing structured data correctly can significantly enhance your online store’s visibility. Here are some benefits:

  • Rich Snippets: These can improve click-through rates by providing potential customers with more information directly in search results.
  • Better Indexing: Search engines can index your pages more effectively, leading to improved rankings.
  • Enhanced User Experience: Clear and informative search results help users find what they need quickly.

Common Causes of Unparsable Structured Data in Magento 2

  1. Incomplete Schema Markup: Missing essential fields like price, availability, or itemCondition can cause errors.
  2. Improper Configuration: Incorrect settings in Magento’s backend can lead to improperly generated structured data.
  3. Outdated Extensions: Using outdated or incompatible extensions may result in conflicts that produce errors.

How to Fix Unparsable Structured Data Issues

Step 1: Validate Your Structured Data

Before making changes, validate your current structured data using tools like Google’s Structured Data Testing Tool. This tool will help you identify specific errors and warnings.

Step 2: Install a Structured Data Module

Consider using a dedicated structured data module for Magento 2. For instance, the OuterEdge Structured Data Module allows you to add schema.org markup easily. After installation, configure the module by navigating to:

Stores > Configuration > outer/edge > Structured Data

Here, you can enable structured data for various pages (product, CMS, contact) and customize attributes like brand and product weight13.

Step 3: Ensure All Required Fields are Present

Make sure that all required fields are included in your structured data markup. For product pages, essential properties include:

  • @type
  • name
  • sku
  • description
  • image
  • offers (including price and availability)
  • aggregateRating

If any of these are missing, update your product information accordingly.

Step 4: Check for Conflicting Markup

If you have multiple types of structured data on a single page (like product and review markup), ensure they do not conflict. Use only one type of markup per item to avoid confusion for search engines6.

Step 5: Update Extensions and Themes

Ensure that all extensions and themes are up-to-date. Outdated components may not generate structured data correctly. Regularly check for updates from reputable developers5.

Step 6: Test After Changes

After making changes, revalidate your structured data using the testing tool mentioned above. This step will confirm whether the issues have been resolved.

Best Practices for Structured Data in Magento 2

  • Use JSON-LD format: This format is recommended by Google for its simplicity and effectiveness.
  • Keep it Simple: Avoid overly complex structures; stick to essential fields.
  • Regular Monitoring: Regularly check Google Search Console for any new errors or warnings related to structured data.

Conclusion

Fixing unparsable structured data issues in Magento 2 is essential for optimizing your online store’s visibility and performance. By validating your markup, ensuring all required fields are present, and using reliable modules, you can enhance your site’s SEO significantly.

Regular monitoring and updates will help maintain the integrity of your structured data, ensuring that search engines can parse it effectively.

By following these guidelines, you can improve not only your site’s SEO but also the overall user experience for potential customers visiting your Magento store.
