What is driving the popularity of voice search?

Accessibility

Voice search appeals to certain groups of people who find it difficult or impossible to type their search queries, including:

  •  People with physical disabilities
  •  People with cognitive or learning disabilities
  •  People who are temporarily unable to use a keyboard or mouse, such as those with a broken arm.

Speed

Voice search allows users to quickly enter information and issue commands to their devices, making it faster than manual input.

Convenience

Voice search is available to users on the go, no matter where they are or what they are doing.

Smart speakers also employ AI that analyses the shopping experience of your visitors and provides personalized results based on their preferences. For example, if your Magento 2 store sells pet products and the AI recognizes that a customer regularly buys dog food, it may remind them to add it to their shopping cart. How does voice search affect Magento 2 SEO? How to optimize your website to voice search?

When it comes to voice search, the length and format of keywords can vary from those used in text searches. As a result, different websites may appear at the top of search engine results pages (SERPs).

To ensure your e-commerce site stays relevant, here are 5 tips to help you Optimize Magento 2 Store for Voice Search:

Tip 1: Mobile Optimization

As the global share of mobile sales in e-commerce is expected to reach 70% of total sales by 2020, according to Statista, and with 27% of users worldwide using voice search on mobile devices, mobile optimization is crucial to maintaining a top ranking in voice search results.

Here are some steps to optimize your Magento 2 store for mobile:

1. Develop a mobile version of your store

2. Test and improve the mobile loading speed

3. Ensure optimal user experience through design testing

4. Synchronize desktop and mobile responses, such as configuring consistent redirects

5. Include all mobile pages in your sitemap and verify they are visible to Googlebot

6. Maintain consistency between desktop and mobile content and metadata

7. Re-test mobile speed to ensure changes haven’t negatively impacted it.

Additionally, consider utilizing PWA technology or the AMP extension to create lightweight mobile pages for your website. Pages created using AMP are 85% faster than non-AMP pages, and the AMP extension allows for the creation of AMP versions for any CMS page.

Tip 2: Achieve Google’s Zero Results Blocks

Ranking at the top of the search results, or a “zero result,” means that Google considers your answer to be the best and will read it out loud to voice searchers. This block was created to allow users to see the answer without having to click on a website link, conserving Google’s resources for indexing millions of pages.

Here are a few suggestions on how to achieve zero results:

  •  Utilize informational keywords and queries that already have zero results;
  •  Incorporate the main keyword in the question you’re looking to answer;
  •  Provide a concise and relevant answer to that question, using lists and bullet points.

Tip 3: Prioritize a Semantic Approach Over Keyword Stuffing

With the advent of Google’s Hummingbird algorithm, context, and long-tail keywords have become increasingly important for improving search rankings. This algorithm prioritizes natural language, with the goal of making online interactions more human-like. Most SEO experts agree that adhering strictly to keyword usage, especially in regard to voice search, is outdated and ineffective. Instead, you should focus on your visitors’ needs and the task that your content should solve.

When it comes to voice search queries, they tend to be longer than text-based ones and often include question words like “how,” “why,” and “where.” This makes prioritizing a semantic strategy even more crucial.

Tip 4. Focus on Speed Optimization

Your website’s load time can play a significant role in retaining or losing visitors. Research shows that over 50% of customers will abandon a website if it takes more than 3 seconds to load. When it comes to voice search, users expect even faster results. To optimize your website’s speed, follow these steps:

1. Test your website using Google PageSpeed Insight to assess the current speed;

2. Analyze the results and use the recommendations to make necessary improvements;

3. Re-test your website to ensure that the changes have had a positive impact.

Some common speed optimization tips include resizing images, deferring offscreen images, and minifying CSS and JS files. You can use a tool like Google Page Speed Optimizer to help with these tasks. This plugin optimizes JS, CSS, and HTML code resizes pictures for mobile devices without sacrificing quality, and offers lazy loading for JS files.

For Magento 2 specifically, consider following the beginner’s guide for improving load speed, implementing the 4 main javascript optimization techniques, and leveraging browser caching.

Tip 5. Re-evaluate your Customer Journey Map (CJM) with voice search in mind

To maximize conversions, it’s important to understand the behavior of voice searchers and how it differs from text searches. By analyzing the voice purchase funnel, you can uncover the steps customers take before making a purchase and identify where they may be dropping off. Without a re-evaluated CJM, it will be difficult to understand the best ways to convert these customers.
