How to Reindex in Magento 2

Published on: January 20, 2023

Reindexing in Magento 2 is a vital process that involves updating the index tables in the database to reflect the current state of data in the system. This is particularly important because certain data such as product prices and stock levels can undergo frequent changes and the index tables need to be regularly updated to ensure their accuracy. 

Before commencing the reindexing process, it is imperative to ensure that you properly set up Magento 2. Subsequently, you can proceed to follow the steps provided below to gain insights into how to reindex in Magento 2.

Steps to Reindex in Magento 2

Step 1: 

Log in to the Magento 2 Admin Panel.

Step 2: 

On the Admin sidebar, go to System > Tools > Index Management.

Step 3:

On the Index Management page, you will see a list of all the indexers in your Magento 2 store. You can select one or more indexers to reindex by selecting the checkbox next to the indexer name.

Step 4:

Click on the “Actions” button at the top of the page, and select “Reindex Data” from the drop-down menu.

Step 5:

Magento 2 will now start reindexing the selected indexers. The process may take a few minutes to complete, depending on the size of your store and the number of indexers you are re-indexing.

Step 6:

Once the reindexing process is complete, you will see a message at the top of the page indicating that the reindexing process has been successfully completed.

STEP: 7

You can also reindex from the command line by running the command:

php bin/magento indexer:reindex

It’s also worth noting that in some cases, Magento 2 may automatically reindex data when certain actions are performed. Examples of this include when products are updated or when new orders are placed. However, in other cases, such as when making bulk updates to products, it may be necessary to manually reindex data to ensure that the index tables are accurate. 

Conclusion:

Reindexing in Magento 2 involves updating the index tables in the database to accurately reflect the current state of data in the system. The steps mentioned above can help you easily reindex your Magento 2 store and ensure that the index tables are always accurate and up-to-date. Regularly reindexing your store is recommended to ensure optimal performance and accuracy. If you encounter any issues with your store, such as slow performance or inaccurate data display, reindexing is often one of the primary steps to troubleshoot the problem.
