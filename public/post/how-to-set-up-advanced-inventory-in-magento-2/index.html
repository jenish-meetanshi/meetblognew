How to Set Up Advanced Inventory in Magento 2?

Published on: May 16, 2023

If you’re wondering how to set up advanced inventory in Magento 2, you’re in luck. Magento 2 provides exceptional features for managing stock, including advanced settings in addition to global stock options.

With the help of Magento’s advanced inventory, you can efficiently manage inventory on a per-product basis and keep track of quantities for multiple products.

Magento 2 offers two ways to configure advanced inventory depending on your needs. You can either use the short form to exclude stock management or the long form to include it. The difference lies in the level of options available for configuring each product.

This article will guide you through the process of configuring Advanced Inventory for Magento 2 inventory using both methods.

Step-by-Step Guide to Set Up Advanced Inventory in Magento 2

To set up Advanced Inventory in Magento, follow these steps

1. Go to Catalog > Products and select the product you want to configure.

2. Click on the corresponding button under Quantity.

Exclude Stock Management

1. Set the “Manage Stocks” field to “No.”

2. Fill out the “Minimum Quantity Allowed in Shopping Cart” field.

3. Specify the “Maximum Quantity Allowed in Shopping Cart.”

4. Choose whether to enable quantity increments.

5. If you enabled quantity increments, enter the number of increments in the “Quantity Increments” field.

6. After filling out all the required fields, click “Done” and save the product.

Include Stock Management

1. Set the quantity of the product.

2. Fill in the “Out-of-Stock Threshold” field to trigger out-of-stock notifications, if desired.

3. Specify the “Minimum Quantity Allowed in Shopping Cart.”

4. Complete the “Maximum Quantity Allowed in Shopping Cart.”

5. In the “Qty Uses Decimals” field, select “Yes” to allow adding quantity values with decimals (e.g. 4.26 lb) or “No” to use only whole numbers.

6. Choose whether to allow multiple boxes for shipping.

7. Move to the “Backorders” field and select one of three options: “No Backorders,” “Allow Qty Below 0,” or “Allow Qty Below 0 and Notify Customer.”

8. Set the threshold for the “Notify for Qty Below” notification.

9. Select “Enable Qty Increments” to allow for quantity increments or “No” to disable it.

10. Fill in the “Qty Increments” field, if enabled.

11. Set the stock status.

12. Click “Done” to save your configuration.

And that’s it for the configuration of the short and long form of the Magento advanced inventory. Just a few steps are required for a better inventory management environment.  

Note – you can enable the “Use Config Settings” option, which will automatically fill in all the fields with their default values.

In addition to setting up advanced inventory management for your products, Magento 2 also allows you to configure low-stock notifications to help you stay on top of your inventory levels. These notifications can be sent to the Magento 2 admin when a product’s stock falls below a certain threshold, allowing you to take action to replenish your stock and avoid running out of products.

Conclusion

If you’re looking to efficiently manage your stock on a per-product basis, setting up Magento 2 advanced inventory can be a game-changer. While the process may seem complex, this step-by-step guide can help you configure advanced inventory using either the short or long-form method based on your needs. With the ability to include or exclude stock management, you’ll have complete control over the fields, thresholds, and options to optimize your inventory management.

By utilizing Magento 2 advanced inventory features, you can streamline your business processes and take control of your stock. Don’t miss out on the benefits of this powerful tool – follow this guide on how to set up advanced inventory in Magento 2 today.
