How to Use Advanced Reporting Functionality in Magento 2: A Comprehensive Guide

Magento 2 Advanced Reporting is a free tool that is only available for its installations. However, there are some prerequisites you should be aware of to use it effectively for your online store.

Who can use Magento 2 Advanced Reporting and Requirements?

Here are the requirements:

1. Your website must run on a public web service.

2. A valid SSL certificate for your domain.

3. A Magento store that has been installed or upgraded without any errors.

4. A single base currency since the time of installation (Advanced Reporting only works for Magento installations that use a single base currency).

5. Correct Base URL settings in the Magento configuration, with the store view pointing to a secure URL and “Use Secure URLs” enabled on both the storefront and in the admin panel.

6. A successful creation of the Magento crontab at the store’s backend, with no issues with the cron jobs on the server.

Please note that it may take up to a day for Reporting to collect and analyze data for How to use Advanced Reporting functionality in Magento 2. To avoid system overload during peak times, it’s recommended to set a preferred time of day for data collection.

How to use Magento 2 Advanced Reporting

Step 1: Enable Advanced Reporting in the Store’s Backend

To activate How to use Advanced Reporting functionality in Magento 2, follow these steps:

  1. Navigate to Stores > Settings > Configuration in the Magento store’s backend.
  2. Select Advanced Reporting in the General section.
  3. Configure settings such as enabling the Advanced Reporting Service, setting a preferred time for data collection, and personalizing analytics for your industry.
  4. Click “Save Config” when finished.

Remember to verify your subscription status and ensure your installation meets all necessary conditions.

Step 2: View the Results in the Admin Panel

Now that you’ve completed the setup, it’s time to see the results of Magento 2 Advanced Reporting. To access the dashboard, go to Reports > Business Intelligence > Advanced Reporting in the Admin Panel.

The dashboard consists of three main tabs: Orders, Customers, and Products. Each tab provides various reports to help you understand your business performance.

Orders: Includes 12 different reports on metrics like Revenue, AOV, Tax, and Shipping fees.

Customers: Contains 4 reports on Unique Customers, New Registered Accounts, Top Coupon Users, and Customer KPI

Access Data resources from the Advanced Reporting dashboard

In the Magento 2 Reporting dashboard, users can quickly access the Data Resources by clicking on the Additional Resources button located in the upper-right corner. The Data Resources offer helpful information through three main sections: Thought Leadership, Support, and Business Intelligence. These resources provide valuable insights and guidance for businesses using the Reporting feature.
